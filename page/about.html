About Me

Hi, I’m Laura, a 27-year-old French islander at heart, creative thinker, and movement enthusiast.

I was born in France but raised in New Caledonia, where I grew up surrounded by turquoise water, palm trees, and a strong sense of curiosity about the world. At 18, my curiosity led me to Singapore, where I studied illustration and concept art. That adventure eventually brought me to Wellington, New Zealand, where I studied graphic design at Yoobee Colleges before completing a Bachelor in Media Design at Victoria University of Wellington.

My Creative Journey

My career has always been about blending creativity with problem-solving. I started out as a web designer and Webflow developer at Psychoactive Studios, where I worked for three years helping brands bring their digital ideas to life. Today, I’m a Creative Developer at KISSLabs, a boutique design studio with a drive to “Keep it simple stupid,” making digital design both beautiful and simple.

Beyond design, I also studied health coaching and earned two diplomas in personal training, because health and movement are just as much a part of my life as design. For me, creativity flows best when the body and mind are both well taken care of.

Movement, Growth and Energy

I’m slightly hyperactive and almost always on the move. Whether I’m walking to work, running around my suburb, lifting weights at the gym, or training for my next challenge, movement is my reset button. I once ran a half marathon and decided long-distance running wasn’t my thing. Now, I’m training for a HYROX competition, which is a mix of strength, endurance, and grit that feels much more my style.

I’ve also been diving into fascia-focused training and deep core work to help balance out my hypermobility and scoliosis. My workouts aren’t just about performance, they’re also about resilience, rehab, and reconnecting to my body.

What I Love

  • Books: I’m a huge reader and love self-help, psychology, fiction, science, and holistic health.
  • Food: I love cooking everything from scratch. Right now, my big obsession is fermentation, with kombucha and kimchi experiments always bubbling in my kitchen.
  • Creativity: From illustration to design, I’m passionate about creative expression. Recently, I’ve taken up knitting as a way to occupy my hands, and it’s an incredible stress reliever. Research suggests hands-on crafts can support neuroplasticity.
  • Video Games: Games are another big passion of mine. My favorites include the Borderlands series, Divinity: Original Sin and Original Sin 2, and storytelling games like Firewatch and Life is Strange. I also love puzzle-rich worlds like Gris and The Witness. I’ve had long obsessions with Dota 2, World of Warcraft, and survival games such as Minecraft, Conan Exiles, Subnautica, Terraria, and ARK. These days, my obsession is shifting into the world of Magic: The Gathering.
  • Learning: I tend to start a new course every year. Whether it’s a creative skill, a health certification, or a new perspective on life, learning keeps me sharp and inspired.

A Life Between Languages and Cultures

French is my mother tongue, but I’ve been speaking English for over a decade. Living across different countries and cultures has shaped me into someone who adapts easily, stays curious, and values both growth and connection.

I see myself as a blend of creative developer, health nerd, and lifelong learner. I’m driven by curiosity, grounded by movement, and inspired by the idea of building things, whether that’s a website, a training plan, or a fermented recipe in my kitchen.
