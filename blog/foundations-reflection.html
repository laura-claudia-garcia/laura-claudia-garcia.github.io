Foundations reflection

Laura Garcia

3 min read 14.09.2025

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

I can struggle to realise that sometimes I know things that other people don’t, and that those things aren’t just “basic knowledge”.

I struggle to bond with other people in an online environment: I haven’t really been feeling comfortable engaging more with people in my course for the moment. Whereas in other in-person courses I have done in the past, after 2 months of studying, I would have formed friendships and acquaintances.

I would rather do it alone than ask for help due to the online and after hours format of the course: I usually work after my job in the evening while having dinner, and being an introvert, I have usually ran out of social battery by the time I open my computer to study, this means that I can struggle to want to connect with peers and tutors when needing help, and would rather find other ways that don’t involve interacting with a person I don’t know well.

What are the roles of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Developing self-awareness and emotional intelligence is crucial in programming and learning. The ability to know yourself will help customize your learning plan so it works for you and uses your skills and learning modes more efficiently, while avoiding stress and intense negative emotions. But it also helps to understand and work on your weaknesses and triggers to be a better person to work with. Being able to work with others is a big part of programming. Efficient collaboration includes the ability to empathise with others, communicate well and resolve conflict in a positive way.

What has surprised you the most about Human Skills learning?

How intimate the questions were: I wasn’t super comfortable sharing such deep thoughts publicly on the internet and with people I have never met before in person. The lack of human contact in the online-course makes this experience very uncomfortable for more introverted people.

Subject focus: I knew there was a human skills part of this course but I didn’t know we would be putting such emphasis on it. It feels a bit out of balance compared to the tech skills we’re learning. In a development course, I was hoping we would be focusing more on the coding aspect of things, with human skills being a bonus. If people need to become better humans, they should do so in a special course in my opinion.

What were the most challenging aspects of Human Skills learning?

Self-reflections and research: since I struggled to see the point of certain subjects, I really struggled to motivate myself to look deeper into certain subjects.

Writing blogs: since english is my second language, I found writing long articles quite challenging, but I liked the opportunity to develop this skill.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Helping people become better humans and easier people to work with will help them be more employable. In a world where most people have the ability to learn coding, what really sets someone apart can be their ability to communicate, work with other people and resolve conflict in a positive way thanks to a higher degree of emotional intelligence.

Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time?

I did a little bit because I already have studied most of these subjects in previous courses I have undertaken as a Health and Exercise coach.

Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

I find it is good to set a base of how people are meant to be working together in the bootcamp, but I do think the emphasis on human skills was a bit too heavy in my opinion. I would have liked to do a bit more coding, a bit less writing about human skills.
