Emotional Intelligence

Have you ever met someone who was academically brilliant but extremely hard to work with? The missing ingredient could possibly have been Emotional Intelligence. But what exactly is it?

Emotional intelligence or emotional quotient (EQ) is the capacity of someone to understand and manage their own emotions, empathise with others and build strong relationships. EQ is a key factor in achieving successful careers, building meaningful relationships, and fostering a healthy relationship with ourselves. The good news is EQ can be learned through self-exploration and practice.

How to learn EQ?

Learning EQ can be approached by progressing through four categories: self-management, self-awareness, social awareness, and relationship management.

Self Management

Learning to manage stress is the most important step to develop EQ. When we are overly stressed, we can become overwhelmed and lose our capacity to control ourselves. Stress management helps us stay emotionally present and maintain clear thinking under pressure.

Sadly, most people experience stress daily and don’t realise the negative impact it has on mental, physical and relational health. Chronic stress is linked to high blood pressure, low immunity, high blood sugar, anxiety, depression and faster ageing to name a few.

To achieve greater balance, you can experiment with techniques such as breathing exercises, journaling, learning to say no, practicing gratitude, making time for social connection and fun, movement, and practicing a healthy lifestyle (getting enough sleep, avoiding caffeine, alcohol, cigarettes, prioritising a whole food diet, drinking enough water).

It is important to learn to manage stress to feel more comfortable confronting stronger and unpleasant emotions.

Self awareness

Self awareness begins by learning to identify our emotions and behaviours so we can manage them in a positive way. To control impulsive feelings and behaviours, we first need to notice they arise, which starts paying attention to our internal world.

Useful tools to identify and reconnect to our emotions are mindfulness and journaling. Practicing mindfulness is done through the practice of full presence, without negative judgment, sensing the physical effect of a said emotion (facial expression or bodily sensations), and analysing our reaction to them. Do you feel it in your chest or in your abdomen? Is there a train of thoughts that comes back when you feel a certain emotion? Do you tend to react the same way to a certain trigger? How strong is the emotion?

Then we can use this information to make decisions about our reactions and behaviours. This is an amazing tool to adapt to the changing circumstances of our fast moving world.

Social Awareness

Social awareness allows you to identify and interpret the verbal and non-verbal of others. To build social awareness you need to practice mindfulness, be in full presence to better pick up on both verbal and non-verbal cues.

A great way to increase social awareness is to practice active listening. Being fully present with the person, and avoiding interrupting when they talk.

Don't be scared to role play with trusted people to practice and get feedback. This can be especially useful when preparing for tough conversations.

Relationship management

Emotional and social awareness are required to develop and maintain great relationships. In order to maintain good relationships, you have to be able to be aware of and manage your emotions and behaviours, communicate effectively, be able to manage and defuse conflict. A great mindset shift is instead of being afraid of conflict, seeing it as an opportunity for growth.

Feedback is important both for personal and career growth, you can practice your relationship management by practicing giving constructive feedback. You can use the ASK (Actionable, Specific, Kind) framework to give good feedback.

What is the difference between EQ and IQ?

Intellectual ability or Intelligence quotient (IQ) is the capacity of someone to perform logic reasoning, problem-solving and analytical skills. It can be measured with IQ tests. But a high IQ doesn’t mean that someone will be more successful or have a higher quality of life, especially if they have a low EQ. Someone with a high IQ that has a poor ability to manage their stress for tests and exams, is likely not going to perform well.

In summary

Emotional intelligence is a must have to be able to work efficiently and positively with others. It is a skill that can be developed through the practice of mindfulness, self-discovery and lots of practice.
