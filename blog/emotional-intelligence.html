Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence or emotional quotient (EQ) is the capacity of someone to understand and manage their own emotions, empathise with others and build strong relationships. EQ is an important factor to achieve successful careers, relationships, including and especially the relationship with ourselves, and the good news is that it can be learnt.

How to learn EQ?

Learning EQ can be broken down in 4 categories or levels: stress management, self-management, self-awareness, social awareness, and relationship management.

Stress Management

Learning to manage stress is the most important step to achieving EQ. When people are overly stressed, they get overwhelmed and tend to lose their capacity to control themselves. Stress management helps us learn to stay emotionally present without letting stress override our thoughts and self-control.

Sadly, nowadays most people experience stress on a daily basis and don’t realise the negative impact it can have on their mental, physical and relational health. Chronic stress is linked to high blood pressure, low immunity, high blood sugar, anxiety, depression and faster ageing to name a few.

Here are a few activities to manage and reduce stress levels: breathing exercises, journaling, learning to say no, practicing gratitude, making time for social connection and fun, movement, and practicing a healthy lifestyle (sleeping enough, avoiding caffeine, alcohol, cigarettes, prioritising a whole food diet, drinking enough water).

It is important to learn to manage stress to feel more comfortable confronting stronger and unpleasant emotions.

Self management and self awareness

The first step to self awareness is to learn to identify our emotions and behaviour to then be able to manage them in a healthy/positive way. To control impulsive feelings and behaviours, we first need to know they exist, and this can be done by first paying attention to our internal world.

Useful tools to identify and reconnect to our emotions are mindfulness and journaling. Practicing mindfulness is done through the practice of full presence, without negative judgment, sensing the physical effect of a said emotion (facial expression or bodily sensations), and analysing our reaction to them. Do you feel it in your chest or in your abdomen? Is there a train of thoughts that comes back when you feel a certain emotion? Do you tend to react the same way to a certain trigger? How strong is the emotion?

Then we can use this information to make decisions about our reactions and behaviours. This is an amazing tool to adapt to the changing circumstances of our fast moving world.

Social Awareness

Social awareness allows you to identify and interpret the verbal and non verbal of others. To build social awareness you need to practice mindfulness, be in full presence to better pick up on both verbal and non verbal cues.

Relationship management

Emotional and social awareness are required to develop and maintain great relationships. In order to maintain good relationships, you have to be able to be aware of and manage your emotions and behaviours, communicate effectively, be able to manage and defuse conflict. A great mindset shift is instead of being afraid of conflict, seeing it as an opportunity for growth.

What is the difference between EQ and IQ?

Intellectual ability or Intelligence quotient (IQ) is the capacity of someone to perform logic reasoning, problem-solving and analytical skills. It can be measured with IQ tests. But a high IQ doesn’t mean that someone will be more successful or have a higher quality of life, especially if they have a low EQ. Someone with a high IQ that has a poor ability to manage their stress for tests and exams, is likely not going to perform well.
