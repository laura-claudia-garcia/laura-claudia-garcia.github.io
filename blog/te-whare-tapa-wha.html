Te Whare Tapa Whā

Te Whare Tapa Whā is a model of the 4 dimensions of wellbeing developed by Tā Mason Durie in 1984 to provide a Māori perspective on health.

The 4 pillars of wellbeing are:

  • Taha tinana (physical wellbeing)
  • Taha hinengaro (mental wellbeing)
  • Taha wairua (spiritual wellbeing)
  • Taha whānau (family wellbeing)

And the foundation: Whenua (land, sea, natural world).

The wharenui is a symbol of these 4 dimensions with 4 walls and a foundation.

Taha tinana

Feeling good in your body, resting, nourishing and strengthening it to be able to withstand life’s challenges while supporting mental health.

Taha hinengaro

Feeling good in your mind and heart involves taking care of your physical wellbeing, eating a nutrient-dense diet, and engaging in stress-relieving strategies.

Taha wairua

Connection to your belief, faith or connection to yourself and the world, experienced through beliefs, values, traditions and practices supporting self-awareness. It helps us feel connected to a community, our family and ourselves.

Taha whānau

The people that make you feel like you belong, who care about you and share your life, whether family, friends or colleagues. Connecting with whānau gives you a sense of purpose, connection and wellbeing.

Whenua

Your health is connected to the health of your land and environment. Connection to nature is greatly beneficial for mental health and wellbeing.

Wellbeing Plan

Taha tinana:
Engaging in daily movement such as walking to work, going to the gym at lunch time, drinking a minimum of 2L of water a day, and nourishing myself with a nutrient-dense diet.

Taha hinengaro:
Engaging in mindfulness and de-stressing practices such as breathing exercises.

Taha wairua:
Calling my partner living overseas every day after work, seeing friends at least once a week, calling my parents at least once a week.

Taha whānau:
Keeping a gratitude journal.

Whenua
Going for a walk or hike in nature without headphones on to listen to nature.
