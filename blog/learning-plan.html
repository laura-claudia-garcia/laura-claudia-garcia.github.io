Learning Plan

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

I would like to be a fullstack developer in the next 4 to 5 years to progress my career. I started my career as a graphic designer in my 20s. This led me to become a web designer and webflow developer. My background in graphic design provided me with a strong eye for detail, user experience and aesthetically driven solutions which can be especially useful to develop software products.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning.

I am a curious and hard-working perfectionist, with a deep passion for design and continued-growth. I try to keep growing and learning more about things that I love to grow in the areas that bring me joy and fulfillment, but I sometimes struggle to know when to stop/when something is “good enough”. I am very industrious, which means that I also tend to get annoyed when people working with me seem to be doing less or care less about a project. I am quite self-disciplined and organised which makes it easier for me to keep track of project progress and meet deadlines.
As an example, in sprint 1-2 I had no issue sticking with my learning plan (studying a little every day and aiming to publish content to github everyday) but I struggled to not go over the timebox for project-based lessons (e.g. blog styling).
I am currently actively working on sticking to timeboxes (by time-tracking with Toggl) in order to not let my perfectionism get the best of me.

What skills (non-technical/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I am looking forward to working in collaboration with other classmates in the bootcamp. I tend to have issues working in teams when people don’t communicate or say what they said they’ll do. I would like to be better at working through obstacles in a positive and productive way without getting emotional.
A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include: An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, Facilitators and industry/community representatives; What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you.
I will ensure that I work productively by studying every evening and a little more during weekends. This ensures that I progress and practice new learnings everyday and stay on track with sprint timelines.
I will make sure that I take a break when I need one and ask help when I am stuck to avoid overworking myself.
I will stay on top of communication with classmates and facilitators and respond to messages and announcements in a timely manner (within 12 to 24 hours). I will also foster a safe and inclusive community by making sure I am being respectful of other people’s beliefs and emotions.
If I ever feel stressed, overwhelmed or behind, I will get in touch with a facilitator to put a plan together in order to work through challenges without falling too far behind and avoid affecting other people’s learning experience.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way.

I will reach out for help via discord to my peers and facilitator if I get stuck for more than 1 to 2 hours in order to get unstuck or look at my problem in a different perspective.

A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team.

I am expecting my facilitators to help me get unstuck without giving me solutions, but rather by helping direct me towards ways I haven’t thought about, except if I am really lost and frustrated and might need a little more technical help. I would also expect a facilitator to provide recommendations when I am unsure of what tools, libraries or tech stack to use for a specific outcome.

Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things. For example, you might have whānau commitments like school drop-offs. It's important to plan these in.

I am planning on taking a two week break in September, and I will be going on a trip to Asia in December, but these are all within the course’s holiday breaks.
